Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My longterm goal is to use these skills to help groups I'm in (activist/political) with utilising digital tools to make change. I'm not planning to have a career in tech, though I may pick up work here or there, if it works like that at all.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My biggest strengths will be my adaptability and ability to reflect and self-regulate which will hopefully allow me to adjust and learn quickly during bootcamp, and work well with others. My limitations will be my focus, even though I am stopping work and trying to pare down other commitments, I still have a lot of other stuff going on. Getting to sleep on time when there's not a lot of "downtime" between days will maybe also be a challenge. I note these are different from the limitations I put in my foundations learning plan, which I don't think will be as big of an issue during bootcamp.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I am noticing, probably completely normally, that my brain only has so much capacity to take in new stuff each day, especially if I'm tired from doing other things. So I'm worried about being able to keep up with the work, as some others seem to be more advanced than myself in coding knowledge. Keeping focussed may also be difficult, though the immersive nature of it helps. Getting sick at this time is always a concern. Working with others in a high-pressure environment when others aren't pulling their weight, or I can't because of my technical limitations is also something I'm worried about.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I have a tendency to sometimes be a bit impatient with others when working on a project - I hope I can improve on this. I'm not sure what leadership skills will be practiced in bootcamp, but that is an area I am keen to develop in. Learning when to ask for help and not feel ashamed about it.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

People who are observant and willing to patiently and non-patronisingly step in to help even when I'm not asking as that can be difficult for me. Actually listening to my problems and trying to understand from my perspective rather than jump in with their assumptions of what the problem is.

What are your expectations of yourself in Bootcamp

I will do my best to ask dumb questions so I don't have gaps in my knowledge, as they come up. And, even more difficult for me, seek help outside of group settings or after the fact. I will try not to use my other group work as a way to avoid doing my bootcamp work. I will try and turn up on time every day (this will be a challenge I'm not an early riser). I will try and maintain my 2 hour a day meditation practice which really helps me work better with others and learn with a clear head. I will try and bring my whole self to these 9 weeks despite the adversities.
